How To Use The Wirecutter

Gadget shopping can be difficult at times. But I think I can help.

How Do I Use Your Site?

Pick the category of gadgets that you want to shop in, and then read the descriptions of our handful of choices. We've gone with high end, low end, and what we believe is just right.

What's the best way to find out when you've updated The Wirecutter?

Browse through the Products sections to see the latest and greatest gadgets.

How do you choose your gadgets?

I study materials put forth by gadget companies. I talk to the best journalists who review gadgets. I take all the data I can find from their work and from around the web. And then I make up my mind. Sometimes I agree, other times I recommend a lower or higher tiered gadget depending on what I think most people will need.

What if the Kind of thing I want Isn't on your site?

Email me and let me know if you need some help figuring out a particular buying problem.

This Is the iPod Dock You Want

Logitech S715i
Add to Wish List
Buy On Amazom

Description

Before we recommend an iPod dock, do you mind if I ask why you want an iPod dock? Most decent docks cost at least 100 bucks, and for the same price, you can get a nice wireless speaker that’s easier to manage and that will work with most smartphones and computers through bluetooth signals. They're also a lot more portable.

Of course, docks have the added benefit of charging your iPod or iPhone, too. So, if you really insist on getting one, Logitech’s S715i is the dock to get. Every gadget reviewer we talked to who has tested the S715i seems to be infatuated with it.

It sounds great and includes rechargeable batteries, so you can lug it to the beach. It also includes an infrared remote for volume, power, shuffle, repeat and track changing.

A few tech reviewers complain that the battery-ladden S175i is a little too bulky, but that extra room also gives it superb bass.

There are some pricier and therefore higher-quality iPod docks out there, like the gigantic Altec Lansing Mix iMT800 ($300). But it makes even less sense to burn money on a high-end dock when you could start building out a Sonos wireless music system, which we consider to be the ultimate home music setup.

We also trust Logitech. This Switzerland-based company has been making peripherals for computers and portables since the 1980s. Another great peripherals company we trust is Belkin, but this manufacturer has been shifting away from iPod docks and focusing on—surprise, surprise, Bluetooth-equipped iPod accessories. See why we recommend one of those instead?

Key Specs

  • Sound: Stereo speakers
  • Size (Width, height): 5 inches, 3.2 inches
  • Weight: 1 pound
  • Compatibility: iPhone, iPod
  • Battery life: 10-hour rechargeable internal battery
